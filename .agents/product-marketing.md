# Product Marketing Context — Fengyu WANG (Personal Brand)

*Last updated: 2026-06-19*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** Market strategy, value investing, and engineering — one integrated perspective.
**What it does:** A multilingual portfolio & thought leadership site for Fengyu WANG (also known as Steve Wang / 王丰羽), presenting three integrated disciplines: market strategy & marketing, value investing & research, and full-stack engineering & automation. Each area has its own dedicated page with original content. The site also hosts 40+ bilingual blog posts and a detailed capability tree.
**Product category:** Founder portfolio + thought leadership + service showcase
**Product type:** Personal brand website (static site, HTML/CSS/JS, hosted on GitHub Pages / Cloudflare Pages)
**Business model:** Service provider / independent consultant — site acts as a credibility asset and lead generation tool

## Target Audience
**Target companies:** Early-stage startups, SMBs, agencies, and consulting firms in Hong Kong, China, and globally that need cross-functional talent (strategy + tech)
**Decision-makers:** Founders, CTOs, marketing directors, and project leads looking for a versatile contributor who can handle both strategic and execution-level work
**Primary use case:** Project credibility check — prospects visit the site to assess Fengyu's breadth, depth, and legitimacy before reaching out
**Jobs to be done:**
- "Prove this person has real marketing experience, not just theory"
- "Show me they can actually build things, not just talk about them"
- "Give me a reason to trust them with my project"

## Personas
Not applicable — personal brand site, single individual.

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Hiring managers and potential clients struggle to find one person who can credibly bridge marketing strategy, investment analysis, AND software engineering — most candidates specialize in only one.
**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Pure marketers can't evaluate technical feasibility or build tools
- Pure engineers lack strategic and business context
- Pure investors offer no tangible deliverable
**What it costs them:** Multiple hires, communication overhead between specialists, slower decision-making
**Emotional tension:** "I need someone who understands the big picture AND can ship code — but that combination is nearly impossible to find."

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** Other hybrid consultant / freelancer portfolio sites (developers who also do strategy, or marketers who also code) — these are rare
**Secondary:** Specialized freelancers on Upwork, Fiverr, Toptal — narrow expertise, one domain only
**Indirect:** Full-service agencies (more expensive, slower, higher overhead) or hiring full-time employees (longer commitment, more risk)

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- **Three-discipline integration:** Marketing strategy + value investing + full-stack engineering — not as a tagline but as demonstrated capability with dedicated pages and 40+ blog posts
- **Bilingual depth:** Full content parity in English, Simplified Chinese, and Traditional Chinese across all pages and 40+ blog posts
- **Thought leadership content:** 40+ original articles covering business strategy, technology, investing, career, and philosophy — not AI-generated filler
- **AI-native:** Uses and writes about AI tools strategically — not hype, but practical workflows
- **Transparent capability tree:** Openly shows skill levels (proficient/familiar/future/decline) including what they WON'T do — builds trust through honesty
**How we do it differently:** Most personal sites show one identity (developer OR marketer OR investor). This one shows all three as a coherent whole.
**Why that's better:** Decision-makers only need one conversation, not three separate vendor evaluations.

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** Frustration with specialists who can't see the bigger picture; communication breakdown between marketing, finance, and engineering teams
**Pull:** One person who "gets" all three domains and can produce tangible output in each
**Habit:** Tendency to default to hiring separate specialists for each function
**Anxiety:** "If I hire one person for three roles, will they be good enough at each?"
