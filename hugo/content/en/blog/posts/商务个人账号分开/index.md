Running a business and creating content are not in conflict. In fact, they complement each other. This is not an either-or choice. It is a question of resource allocation.

From a logical perspective, these two paths not only avoid conflict, they reinforce each other.

Enterprise services target the B2B market: building software, solving problems for companies, earning money through professionalism and reliability, with stable income, high customer value, and strong repeat business. Content creation targets the C2C market: making videos, writing opinions, building a personal brand, earning through influence and reach.

These two paths serve completely different markets and customer bases. Therefore, there is no risk of customer confusion.

What truly needs to be considered is the allocation of energy and roles. Doing two things at once inevitably divides one focus. But viewed from another angle, these two activities can form a positive cycle. Content creation can become a channel for attracting enterprise clients. Enterprise service cases and insights can becomematerial for content creation. Many successful business influencers have walked this path.

The key is to find the ratio that suits you best. If you need stable cash flow, prioritise enterprise services and treat content creation as a side line. If you are in the accumulation phase, prioritise content and use enterprise services to maintain basic income. This ratio can be adjusted dynamically.

The worst approach is to choose neither because you cannot decide. Start moving, adjust along the way. That is far more effective than standing still and thinking.
