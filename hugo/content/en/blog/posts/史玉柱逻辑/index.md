Shi Yuzhu business logic can be condensed into one sentence: do not educate, do not debate, do not change. Only identify, only satisfy, only close the deal.

The core insight behind this approach has three layers.

The first insight: people are very difficult to persuade. Trying to change someone worldview has infinite cost. Rather than spending time educating those who do not agree with you, find those who already agree.

The second insight: do not argue with clever people. Give ordinary people the sense of security they want. Shi Yuzhu strategy was never about convincing through logic. It was about using simple, repetitive, emotionally charged messages to penetrate the target audience mind.

The third insight: cognitive stratification is real. Different groups make decisions differently. High-cognition people decide based on logic and data analysis. Ordinary people decide based on emotion and trust. Using the wrong strategy is inefficient. Using the right strategy is a form of dimensionality reduction.

This logic corresponds to a classic theory in academia: market segmentation and positioning theory. The idea is not to serve everyone, but to find the best-matched audience and communicate with them in the way that suits them best. Shi Yuzhu uniqueness lies in how he simplified this theory to its extreme: no complex market analysis is needed. Just know clearly who your customers are and what they want.

The boundary condition for this logic is that its effectiveness depends on the market and audience you choose. If you are in a high-end market dealing with rational decision-makers, this approach may backfire. But if you are in a mass market dealing with ordinary consumers, its effectiveness has been repeatedly proven.
