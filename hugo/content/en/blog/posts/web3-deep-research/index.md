This article is a systematic analysis of the Web3 industry from seven dimensions.

**Part I: The Core Essence and Underlying Logic of Web3**

Web3 is a marketing narrative first, technological innovation second. It is not an internet generational disruption — it is a financial and organizational experiment riding on blockchain infrastructure. The real value lies not in "decentralization for its own sake" but in re-examining existing trust models and finding scenarios where tokenization, programmability, and disintermediation deliver measurable improvements.

**Part II: Global Web3 Regulatory Systems and Compliance Research**

Complete decentralization cannot be implemented in the real world. Regulators globally have converged on a pragmatic approach: anti-money laundering, investor protection, and stablecoin reserves. Hong Kong's 2025 Stablecoin Ordinance is analyzed as a benchmark policy. Global regulatory divergence and mainstream enterprise compliance approaches are compared across the US, EU, Asia, and the Middle East.

**Part III: Web3 Business Value and Benchmark Cases**

Much current Web3 activity is "new wine in old bottles" — value returns to real-world scenarios. The Polymarket prediction market is analyzed as a benchmark case demonstrating real-world utility and empirical value.

**Part IV: Traditional Large Enterprises Entering Web3**

Core enterprise advantages, implementation directions that are actionable and low-risk, risk isolation principles, and foundational technical concepts are covered for traditional enterprises exploring Web3 adoption.

**Part V: Overall Web3 Development Trends, Opportunities, and Challenges**

Mainstream development trends and core industry challenges including scalability, regulatory uncertainty, and institutional adoption hurdles.

**Part VI: Five In-Depth Research Topics**

1. Cryptocurrencies and Stablecoins
2. Real-World Assets (RWA) Tokenization
3. AI + Web3 Convergence
4. On-Chain Governance and DAO Evolution
5. Enterprise Blockchain Implementation Pathways
